Study Abroad in Copenhagen

One Week Complete

May 27, 2019

Quite a few things have happened since my last post. After traveling by bike, visiting interesting spots around the city, seeing a rave, along with other events, this week has been packed with activities.

Early this week, we went on a guided bike tour throughout the city. It's always crazy to see how many bicycles and how few cars are in Copenhagen. I really liked the tour with how the Swedish guide talked to us about the current politics in Scandinavia (though she may have been a little biased toward Sweden). I now understand how safe and efficient biking is for the city (only 1 fatal wreck in every 2 years), and it was cool to see how bikes have their own roads.

On Friday, we drove to Aarhus to have a technical talk with a Google software engineer. After the talk, we explored the city and visited some incredibly modern and interactive art museums. Personally, I'm not a huge fan of museums, but these places are must-sees for anyone.

One night this weekend, some of us went to a bar to play pool which was a fun experience. After the bar, a couple of friends and I went to a rave to see Slow Magic. I was excited to see this artist since I've listened to his music back home. The show was pretty crazy once it started, and he played plenty of drums and electronic music throughout.

This week, I will be going to the Distortion music festival in Copenhagen, visiting Sweden, along with a bunch of other fun activities.

The First Two Days

May 21, 2019

After two delays and plenty of confusing French, it's safe to say that the flights were a success. Sarcasm aside, the international flight was not like anything I've ever experienced. They had various entertainment options to choose from along with plenty of free drinks and food.

When I finally arrived to the Copenhagen airport, I was surprised with their nice, mall-like airport. We were greeted by DIS and taken by bus to our housing locations. The rooms are very spacious and there's always something buzzing about the city. With little sleep from the flight, I took advantage of the first night to sleep early and explore more the following day.

The culture of Copenhagen slightly shocked me at first in two things: how nice and conserved the Danes are. I really enjoy talking to them even if they don't realize that I can't speak back to them in Danish. The people here are nice whenever you don't look too much like an American tourist.

Overall, it's been a great arrival, and I'm looking forward to seeing what else the city has to offer.
