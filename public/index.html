Study Abroad in Copenhagen

The First Two Days

May 21, 2019

After two delays and plenty of confusing French, it's safe to say that the flights were a success. Sarcasm aside, the international flight was not like anything I've ever experienced. They had various entertainment options to choose from along with plenty of free drinks and food.

When I finally arrived to the Copenhagen airport, I was surprised with their nice, mall-like airport. We were greeted by DIS and taken by bus to our housing locations. The rooms are very spacious and there's always something buzzing about the city. With little sleep from the flight, I took advantage of the first night to sleep early and explore more the following day.

The culture of Copenhagen slightly shocked me at first in two things: how nice and conserved the Danes are. I really enjoy talking to them even if they don't realize that I can't speak back to them in Danish. The people here are nice whenever you don't look too much like an American tourist.

Overall, it's been a great arrival, and I'm looking forward to seeing what else the city has to offer.

Ready for Departure

May 17, 2019

Welcome to my travel blog for my trip to Copenhagen! In just a couple of days, I am leaving for this study abroad trip, and I can't wait to share my experiences on this site.

Currently, this site features an aerial view of Copenhagen as well as a changing background. The background depicts the vibrant colors of houses found throughout the city. I look forward to exploring more of this beautiful city with several goals in mind.

I am particularly excited about this course since we will focus on both front end and back end web development. My goal for this website is to make it as efficient as possible in order to provide an interactive experience for the user.

With that said, be on the lookout for updates to this blog as it will continue to improve during the course!
